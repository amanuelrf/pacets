What Does a Dispatcher Do? A Comprehensive Guide

A truck dispatcher connects drivers with freight. They search for loads, negotiate rates, schedule pickups and deliveries, and communicate with brokers, shippers, and drivers throughout the trip.

Core responsibilities

  • Finding and booking loads that fit the truck type and lane strategy
  • Managing paperwork: rate confirmations, BOLs, and broker requirements
  • Tracking driver hours and location to avoid conflicts and delays

Tools dispatchers use

Load boards, TMS software, mapping, and ELD integrations are common. Good dispatchers also build relationships with brokers to get repeat freight.
